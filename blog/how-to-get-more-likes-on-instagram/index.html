How to Get More Likes on Instagram (Without Paid Ads)

By Dani Edson Internet MarketingSocial MediaFree Download

Getting more likes on Instagram in 2026 comes down to understanding what the algorithm prioritizes and creating content designed to trigger that behavior. While saves and shares have become more important algorithmic signals than likes, likes still matter — they're the primary visible indicator of content quality, and high like counts encourage more people to engage.

Why Likes Still Matter in 2026

Instagram's algorithm uses likes as one of several engagement signals to determine content quality. More importantly, the like count is publicly visible — and high like counts create social proof that encourages new viewers to like, comment, and follow. The goal isn't likes for their own sake; it's creating content that naturally earns them.

1. Post High-Quality Visuals Consistently

Instagram is a visual platform. Content that is visually appealing or instantly recognizable as belonging to a clear aesthetic gets more likes because it stops the scroll. You don't need expensive equipment — you need consistent lighting, clear composition, and a consistent visual identity.

2. Time Your Posts for Maximum Initial Exposure

The first 30–60 minutes after posting are when Instagram decides whether to push your content. Posting when your specific audience is most active (check Instagram Insights) means more people see it immediately, generating more likes in the critical early window.

3. Write Captions That Earn Emotional Responses

Posts with captions that make people feel something — inspired, amused, validated, or surprised — earn more likes than purely informational posts. The like button is an emotional response mechanism, not a logical one.

4. Use Relevant Hashtags

Hashtags put your content in front of people who don't already follow you but are interested in your topic. Using a mix of niche-specific (under 200k posts) and broader hashtags gives you the best exposure across audience segments.

5. Post Reels With Strong Hooks

Reels with high completion rates (people who watch the full Reel) earn disproportionately more likes and shares than Reels that people swipe away from. This is because completing a Reel puts viewers in a "reward" state that makes them more likely to like and engage.

6. Engage With Similar Accounts

Commenting genuinely on posts from accounts in your niche is still one of the most effective organic discovery tactics on Instagram. People who see a thoughtful comment click through to the commenter's profile — and if they like what they see, they follow and like future content.

7. Collaborate With Other Creators

Collab posts appear on both creators' profiles and get liked by both audiences. Even one or two collabs per month with similarly-sized accounts can meaningfully increase your average like count over time.

8. Create "Double-Tap Worthy" Moments

Some content formats are specifically designed to earn likes: satisfying before-and-after transformations, relatable humor, stunning visuals, or the exact moment something unexpected happens. Study your highest-liked posts and identify what they have in common.

9. Reply to Comments to Bring Viewers Back

Every time you reply to a comment, that person gets a notification that brings them back to your post. Many of them will like the post again when they return, or like your reply. Over the lifespan of a post, active reply sessions can add dozens or hundreds of additional likes.

Buffer's research confirms this: accounts that reply to comments see up to 21% higher engagement on Instagram — including likes.

10. Post Regularly (Consistency Over Frequency)

Accounts that post consistently — even at moderate frequency — build algorithmic momentum that sporadic high-volume posters don't. The algorithm learns your schedule and starts showing your content to more people when it expects you to post.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many likes is good on Instagram in 2026?

A healthy benchmark is 1–3% of your follower count per post. An account with 5,000 followers getting 50–150 likes per post is performing well. More important than the absolute number is whether your engagement rate is growing over time.

Why am I getting fewer likes on Instagram?

Common causes: posting at suboptimal times, content that doesn't match current algorithm preferences (static images underperform Reels), inconsistent posting schedule, or a mismatch between your content and your follower demographics.

Does commenting help get more likes?

Yes, in two ways. Commenting on others' posts drives people to your profile, where they may like your content. And replying to comments on your own posts brings commenters back, which often results in additional likes. Both are worth doing consistently.
