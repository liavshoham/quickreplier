How to Get More Views on YouTube in 2026

By Dani Edson Internet MarketingYouTube · GrowthFree Download

YouTube views come from two main sources: search (people looking for specific content) and recommendations (YouTube suggesting your video to people who might enjoy it). Growing views requires optimizing for both — and understanding that engagement is the bridge between the two.

The Two Paths to More YouTube Views

Search views come from people typing keywords into YouTube search. These are predictable and consistent once you rank — a video that ranks on page 1 for "how to make sourdough bread" will keep getting views for years. The downside is that ranking takes time and competition is high for popular topics.

Recommendation views come from YouTube's "Up Next" sidebar and the homepage feed. These are harder to predict but can drive enormous volume very quickly when a video picks up algorithm momentum. Engagement signals — watch time, comments, likes, shares — are what trigger recommendation momentum.

1. Master Your Thumbnails

Thumbnail CTR is the most controllable variable in getting more views. A 1% improvement in CTR can double your views on the same content. Test different thumbnail styles: human faces vs. text-only, bright vs. dark, one dominant image vs. split comparisons. Use YouTube Studio's impression CTR data to track what works for your specific audience.

2. Optimize Titles for Both Clicks and Search

The best YouTube titles in 2026 balance two things: curiosity (making someone want to click) and keywords (helping YouTube understand what the video is about). "I Tried Waking Up at 5AM for 30 Days" is curiosity-driven. "How to Wake Up at 5AM (Without an Alarm)" is search-driven. The best titles combine both: "Why I Wake Up at 5AM Every Day (How to Start)."

3. Front-Load Your Best Content

If the first 2 minutes of your video don't deliver value, most viewers leave — and YouTube registers a poor retention signal that reduces recommendations. Open with your strongest insight, hook, or demonstration, and save background/context for after you've already delivered something valuable.

4. Target Low-Competition Keywords

The fastest path to search views is finding topics with meaningful search volume but low competition (few existing videos with good optimization). Use YouTube search auto-complete to find these "long tail" opportunities — they often convert better because the viewer's intent is very specific.

5. Build a Content Cluster Around Your Best Topics

When you create multiple videos around the same topic (a "cluster"), YouTube starts recommending your other videos to viewers who watched one of them. This dramatically increases your channel's recommendation distribution for that topic area.

6. Promote New Videos Across All Your Platforms

The first 48–72 hours of a video's life are when YouTube is most actively evaluating it for recommendation potential. Driving external traffic (from Instagram, TikTok, email list, community groups) during this window signals high demand, which can trigger the algorithm to push the video more broadly.

7. Improve Watch Time by Removing Slow Sections

Ruthless editing is one of the most effective ways to improve watch time. Use YouTube Studio's audience retention graph to identify exactly where viewers are dropping off in each video, then cut or re-edit those sections in future videos.

8. Reply to Comments to Extend Your Video's Engagement Window

A video that continues generating comments and engagement days or weeks after upload keeps sending positive signals to the algorithm. Creators who maintain active reply sessions after uploading consistently report longer "tail" view counts — the video keeps getting recommended well beyond the initial upload window.

The creators with the highest long-term view counts aren't necessarily the ones with the biggest ad budgets — they're the ones who create genuine conversations around their content. Comment replies are the fastest way to build that.

9. Use YouTube Analytics to Double Down on What Works

YouTube Studio's analytics show you exactly which videos drove the most new subscribers, which had the highest CTR, and which generated the most watch time per view. These are your "winning" formats — make more videos like these, not more like everything else.

10. Create Content That Earns Shares

Views from shares have an outsized impact on YouTube's algorithm because they come from outside the platform. When someone shares your video on WhatsApp, Reddit, or Twitter, YouTube counts that as a strong quality signal. Create content that people feel compelled to share: surprising statistics, genuinely useful tutorials, or emotionally resonant stories.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many views per day is good on YouTube?

This depends entirely on your channel size and niche. For a new channel, 10–50 views per day per video is healthy growth. For an established channel with 10,000+ subscribers, 100–500 views per day per video is a reasonable baseline. What matters most is the trend line, not the absolute number.

Do likes help YouTube views?

Yes. Likes are an engagement signal that YouTube uses to evaluate video quality. More importantly, videos with high like-to-view ratios tend to also have high comment rates and watch time — the combination of all these signals drives recommendation distribution.

How long does it take to get views on a new YouTube channel?

Most new channels see their first 100–1,000 views within the first month from search traffic. Recommendation views typically don't kick in significantly until a channel has established a pattern of consistent uploads and engagement, usually after 3–6 months of regular publishing.
